Why Pick Stocks at All?

Good question:

"Research by Hendrick Bessembinder, finance professor and Francis J. and Mary B. Labriola Endowed Chair in Competitive Business, found that returns from long-term stock investing are positively skewed, meaning that very large returns to a few stocks pull up the average, while most stocks post modest or negative returns". Source

This research is extremely humbling to the industry, and it is imperative that investors understand the data.

What does this mean?

More than half of all the companies in the dataset had negative returns over the period. Data into the future will likely only look worse, as the average life of a company in the S&P500 continues to decline to it's current 15 years from 67 years. Source

Back to the study:

Bessembinder found that just 86 stocks accounted for $16 Trillion in wealth creation; half the entire market return over the last 90 years. 96% of all the stocks simply matched 1-month Treasury Bills.

Bessembinder's research has been extensively covered, and has been published. From 1926 to 2019, net wealth creation of public markets has increased $34.8 Trillion.

The gist is that: all of the wealth creation can be attributed to the thousand top-performing stocks, while the remaining 96 percent of stocks collectively matched one-month T-bills.

Furthermore, data from this study: Source show that - out of 64,000 globally listed securities - from 1990 to December 2020, over 50% of stocks underperform 1 month Treasuries and the top 2.4% of stocks account for all the $75.7Trillion in global net wealth creation. Outside of the US, it is 1.4%.

In this study: Source We can see the reinforcing principle of long term free cash flow generation and low valuations for generating strong returns over time, as with the case of top performing Altria. Long duration assets that generate significant free cash flow that can be returned to shareholders.
